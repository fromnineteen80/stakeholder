## 1. HP's 10-Step Framework for Successful Stakeholder Relations

### PURPOSE (Steps 1-2)
1. **Business Goals**
   - Define how stakeholder relations support organizational objectives
   - Owners: ELT, Business Unit, Country Team

2. **Issue Identification**
   - Assess current relationship status with stakeholders
   - Owners: ELT, CLT, Business Unit, Analyst Team, Stakeholder Relationship Managers

### PLAN (Steps 3-6)
3. **Stakeholder Identification**
   - Map those with influence over business, message, credibility, visibility
   - Owners: Business Unit, Country Team, CLT, Stakeholder Team

4. **Stakeholder Prioritization**
   - Identify those with greatest opportunity/risk impact
   - Owners: Business Unit, Country Team, CLT, Stakeholder Team

5. **Landscape Analysis & Resource Identification**
   - Map prioritized stakeholders with necessary resources
   - Owners: CLT, Analyst Team, Stakeholder Team

6. **Internal Alignment & Governance**
   - Establish oversight for aligned strategy and outreach
   - Owners: Business Unit, Country Team, CLT, Stakeholder Team

### EXECUTE (Steps 7-8)
7. **Launch Campaign**
   - Purposeful communication channels for feedback
   - Sustainable investment in targeted stakeholders
   - Iterative refinement process
   - Owners: CLT, Stakeholder Team, Sustainability, Executive Comms

8. **Targeted Engagement**
   - Executive thought leadership
   - Business/message/impact collaboration
   - Issue resolution
   - Continuous refinement
   - Owners: CLT, Executive Comms, Social Media, Stakeholder Team

### CREATE VALUE (Steps 9-10)
9. **For HP Organization Only**
   - Measure outcomes and value creation
   - Identify unique competitive advantages
   - Project future stakeholder value opportunities
   - Owners: ELT, Business Unit, Country Team, CLT, Stakeholder Team

10. **Shared Value for HP & Target Stakeholders**
    - Collaborative next-step planning
    - Mutual benefit realization
    - Owners: ELT, Business Unit, Country Team, CLT, Stakeholder Team, Targeted Stakeholders

## 5. Todoist UX Principles - Simplicity Excellence

### Core Design Philosophy
**"Don't make me think" - Radical simplicity**

### Key Features to Emulate:
1. **Zero-Friction Task Entry**
   - Single-click task creation
   - Natural language date processing
   - Auto-creation from copy/paste

2. **Clear Affordances**
   - "+" icon with "Add Task" prompt
   - Hover tooltips with shortcuts
   - Visual hierarchy through opacity

3. **Smart Automation**
   - Recurring due dates (daily, weekly, monthly, yearly)
   - Priority levels (P1-P4)
   - Task dependencies

4. **Minimal Interface**
   - No color codes or excessive labels
   - Task + Date + Priority = Complete
   - Focus on single action at a time

5. **Organizational Hierarchy**
   - Projects for major groupings
   - Sections within projects
   - Tags for cross-project themes
   - Filters for custom views

### What NOT to Do (Todoist Lessons):
- Don't hide multi-project view capability
- Don't make sharing features unclear
- Don't add friction to core workflows
- Don't overwhelm with configuration options

## 6. Slack UX Principles - Empathetic Collaboration

### Core Design Philosophy
**"Build with empathy for the end user"**

### Design System (Slack Kit) Principles:

**1. Progressive Disclosure**
- Show only what's needed for current step
- Use overflow menus for secondary actions
- Reveal advanced options when necessary
- Context blocks for supplementary information

**2. Minimize Cognitive Load**
- Focus on simple, common tasks
- Reduce choices users must make
- Smart defaults (e.g., last coffee order)
- Clear visual hierarchy

**3. Message Lifecycle Management**
- Visually rich in the moment
- Condensed after interaction expires
- Update messages after workflow completes
- Consider "reading the room" cues

**4. Contextual Wisdom**
- Respond in appropriate channels
- Private workflows when appropriate
- Use DMs vs public channels intelligently

**5. Design for Scale**
- 5-person to 50,000-person workspaces
- Accommodate guest accounts
- Role-based permissions
- Truncate/abbreviate long lists gracefully

### Slack Development Process:
- **Pair Design**: Two designers riffing together
- **Dogfooding**: 8+ hours/day internal usage
- **Rapid Iteration**: Ship within a day, test, refine
- **Cross-functional Squads**: Designer, developer, PM, support
- **Living Product Briefs**: Evolving context documents
- **Post-Kickoff Freedom**: Explore then formalize constraints

### Typography & Color Evolution:
- Moved from solid purple to linear gradients
- Lighter, more 3D-esque appearance
- Personalization via themes
- 16-color pruned palette for consistency
- Design tokens framework

## 8. UX Best Practices Synthesis

### User-Centered Design Fundamentals:
1. **Know Your Users**
   - Not "users" but "humans"
   - Understand jobs-to-be-done
   - Research before designing
   - Test with real users continuously

2. **Consistency is King**
   - Internal consistency (colors, fonts, buttons)
   - External consistency (web conventions)
   - Predictable interactions
   - Design system enforcement

3. **Accessibility Always**
   - WCAG guidelines compliance
   - High contrast ratios
   - Screen reader compatibility
   - Keyboard navigation

4. **Minimize Cognitive Load**
   - Points, lines, planes (Bauhaus basics)
   - Remove unnecessary elements
   - Guide don't dictate
   - Progressive complexity

5. **Mobile-First Thinking**
   - 50%+ traffic from mobile
   - Touch-friendly targets
   - Responsive across devices
   - Performance optimization

6. **Feedback & Validation**
   - Ask "why" five times
   - Thorough UX research
   - Usability testing
   - Iterate based on data

## 11. Design System Specifications

### Color Palette (from Soappboxx Slide 18):
**Primary Colors:**
- Deep Blue: #3E605A (headers, primary actions)
- Olive Green: #46796F (secondary elements)
- Sage: #BDD9C1 (backgrounds, cards)
- Cream: #FFFFA (text on dark backgrounds)
- Burnt Orange: #B86F56 (alerts, warnings)
- Terracotta: #7F3C2E (errors, critical items)

**Accent Colors:**
- Mustard: #FFE07C (highlights, success states)
- Gray Variations: For text hierarchy and borders

### Typography:
- **Primary Font**: Roboto or Poppins
- **Heading Sizes**: H1 (32px), H2 (24px), H3 (18px)
- **Body Text**: 14-16px
- **Caption/Meta**: 12px
- **Line Height**: 1.5-1.6 for readability

### Component Library Needs:
- Buttons (Primary, Secondary, Tertiary, Danger)
- Form inputs (Text, Textarea, Select, Checkbox, Radio)
- Cards (Stakeholder, Interaction, Task)

- Modals and Dialogs
- Navigation (Top nav, Sidebar, Breadcrumbs)
- Status badges and tags
- Progress indicators
- Date pickers and calendars
